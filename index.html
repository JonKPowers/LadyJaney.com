About Lady the Bad Dog

Lady Janey (AKA "Lady") was born to a family of purebred Blue Tick Beagles in Hardin County, Kentucky. She is bred to hunt by scent, and her prey of choice are rabbits and squirrels. Lady loves nothing more than to track a scent through thick brush and employ her hunting know-how.

Lady's start in life was inauspicious. Soon after birth, she ended up a kill shelter, but was luckily rescued by the Kentucky Humane Society. Her owners first saw her at the St. Matthews Feeders Supply on December 2, 2016, and immediately fell in love. Unfortunately, Lady was quite sick. She had kennel cough and dandruff, and had just gotten over a bout of fleas. This made her future owners even more determined to give her a good home and help her get better. Lady went home with them the next day and they've all been living happily ever since ... just kidding.

True to her Beagle self, Lady has proved to be an overly precocious and curious doglet who craves attention and treats. She especially loves going to the dog park and running furiously with other doggies. She has never met a dog or a human she didn't love at first sight. You will be her friend whether you like it or not. In her mind, Lady is pack leader of all dogs, as well as her human family. Her owners do not dispute this. This is one Beagle who knows her mind and has no qualms about expressing herself.

These days, Lady's primary responsibilities include learning new commands. She has already perfected "sit," "down," and "jump," and has somehow always known how to stand upright on her hind legs. Lady also gives the best doggie kisses. She is frequently described by those who meet her as "really pretty," "lovely," and as having a "great temperament," although Lady's dad frowns upon inflating her ego too much. Nevertheless, it can't be helped. Lady is too special for words -- sociable, intelligent, and loving. In other words, the best kind of bad dog.
